Founding Belief

We built this because teaching deserves better tools.

A student who misses a question isn't always missing the concept in front of them. Sometimes it's something from three months ago. A gap that was never caught, quietly growing.

Teachers already carry enough. They're managing 40 students, running lessons, and trying to figure out who's lost and who's not, often with nothing but a marked paper and intuition.

Assessment should do more than keep score. It should show you exactly where to look.

The Problem

The way we assess students isn't working.

01
We mark answers. We don't diagnose understanding.

A wrong answer tells you a student struggled. It doesn't tell you why. Edurena connects mistakes to the concepts behind them, so you know what to reteach, not just what to re-mark.

02
There's too big a gap between teaching and testing.

Students forget. That's not a character flaw, it's how memory works. Short, regular check-ins reinforce what you've taught before it fades. Edurena makes those check-ins effortless.

03
Large classes make it easy to miss who's falling behind.

When you have thirty or forty students, the quiet ones who are struggling rarely raise their hands. Edurena surfaces those students before it becomes a crisis.

04
Exam integrity is hard to maintain.

Once a question leaves the classroom, you can't control it. Edurena gives you monitoring and proctoring tools that ensure the results you see actually reflect what your students know.

How it works

One question bank. Every kind of assessment.

01
Build your question bank once.
Type, upload, or import your questions. They live in one place and work across every mode, no rebuilding from scratch each term.
02
Choose how you want to use them.
The same questions can become a proctored exam, a live classroom game, or a quiet revision quiz. You decide what fits the moment.
03
See what your class actually understood.
After any assessment, you get a clear picture: which topics landed, which didn't, and which students need a conversation.
